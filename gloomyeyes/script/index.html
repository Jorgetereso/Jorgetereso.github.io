INTRO

The Hiding of the Sun

Shadow Miniature Prologue ~3 MIN
REWRITE

Darkness. A low, distant warmth pulses somewhere far away.

The Gravekeeper appears slowly, emerging from shadow. He carries a small lantern and a handful of moths circle the light. He has nothing else — no stage, no projector. Only what he found around the cemetery.

He sets the lantern down on a flat stone. With slow, careful gestures, he begins to tell the story using the things at hand: a few small puppets carved from bone and wood, the moths drawn to the light, the shadows they throw against the tombs.

As he speaks, the puppets begin to move on their own. The moths form patterns. The shadows take shapes. The cemetery itself becomes the stage.

GRAVEKEEPER

Long ago, the sun chose to hide itself.
It retreated deep inside the mountains, away from everything it once touched.

Without its light, the land began to rot. Forests weakened, animals disappeared, and the balance between humans and nature collapsed.

From that darkness, zombies appeared. Not as monsters, but as the consequence of what had been broken.

Fear spread quickly, and the Uncle turned it into hatred.

He looks at us — really looks at us — for the first time.

GRAVEKEEPER

You are what's left now. The last surviving zombies.
The Uncle put you underground, where things are meant to disappear.
Without the sun, nothing really worked anymore.

He picks up the lantern again. The moths follow.

GRAVEKEEPER

There is only one chance left. Gloomy is still out there.
If there is a change coming, it will start with him.

He pauses, then adds — almost as a side note, but with weight:

GRAVEKEEPER

When in doubt… follow the fireflies.

Source: "Qué el Gravekeeper nos introduzca la historia, usando elementos que tenga a mano, pequeños como una lámpara y unas polillas." + "Ante la duda sigan a las luciérnagas."

SCENE 01

The Dungeons — Execution Order

Underground Cages + Freight Elevator ~14 MIN

We awaken inside zombie bodies, locked inside the Uncle's underground dungeons in old cages. In front of us, humans dressed as rabbits stand guard.

REWRITE

The Uncle enters carrying a cup of coffee. He takes a sip — and immediately gags, spitting it out with disgust.

UNCLE

Who made this disgusting coffee?!

He turns toward the bunny guards, threatening.

UNCLE

Was it you? Or you? Or YOU?!

At that exact moment, Nina enters carrying more coffee. She walks up behind the Uncle with a cheerful little smile and stops just behind him.

NINA

I made it!

The Uncle freezes. His whole demeanor flips instantly — from rage to syrupy affection. This is the manipulator showing his real face.

UNCLE

Nina! I can't believe it! Such talent.
I taste fresh notes — like cypress wood. Light, but complex.
Silky. With a long, lingering aftertaste.

Nina, holding up a small can, reads the label flatly.

NINA

I put shoe polish in it.

The Uncle holds back a visible gag. He swallows hard, smiling through it.

While the Uncle tries to keep his composure, Nina drifts toward our cages. She looks at us — really looks. Curious. Not afraid.

Just as she walks away from him, the Uncle quietly hands the coffee cup to one of the bunny guards. His voice drops to something cold and final.

UNCLE

(deadly quiet) Drink it. Now.

The bunny freezes. Slowly raises the cup. The Uncle stares him down, unblinking. Behind them, Nina has reached the bars of our cage — not noticing.

Nina turns back to the Uncle, almost casually.

NINA

What's going to happen to them?

UNCLE

(without looking at her) Nothing.

NINA

So you're going to release them?

The Uncle slowly turns. His voice drops into something much darker.

UNCLE

They were dead once.
I'm going to put them back where they belong.
This time, deeper. This time, for good.

A beat. Nina watches us through the bars. The Uncle's voice turns ugly.

UNCLE

(simple, cold disgust) Look at them. They're disgusting.
I can't stand the sight of them.

NINA

(quietly, looking right at us) They don't seem so ugly to me. I'm not sure they're so different.

The Uncle starts losing his temper. He grabs the hood of one of the bunny guards and yanks it off to make a point.

UNCLE

Of course they are! We are very different. Aesthetically superior!

He looks at the bunny's face without the hood — and immediately recoils. The bunny without the hood is the most hideous thing imaginable. The Uncle lets out a tiny horrified squeak and shoves the hood back on the bunny's head as fast as he can.

UNCLE

(recovering) They're dangerous. They're a plague. We have to wipe them all out. Every single one.

NINA

They don't look dangerous to me.

UNCLE

(exploding, turning on her) Enough!
I'm sick of your questions. Do you want to end up like them? Do you want to be DEAD?!

NINA

I didn't do anything wrong!!

The Uncle, in pure frustration, lashes out — and punches one of the bunny guards in the face. The one who had nothing to do with anything. The bunny crumples to the floor.

UNCLE

(pointing at Nina) Look what you make me do!

UNCLE

You — take her away!

A bunny grabs Nina and drags her out. She fights, but barely. She knows this fight.

Just before she disappears through the doorway, Nina twists her head back toward us and shouts:

NINA

Someone is waiting for you above!

Her voice cracks. She knows what comes next for her. This is a sacrifice she's making — and she's making it knowingly.

She's gone.

The Uncle turns back toward us. His face is empty.

UNCLE

Kill them. Now.

He walks out without looking back.

Source: Full dungeon scene rewrite from REWRITE 4/2026. Coffee/shoe-polish gag, Nina connected to the Uncle, the Uncle as the violent one (he hits the bunny, not Nina), and Nina's "Someone is waiting above" reframed as her sacrifice — "Pensamiento: Nina ya rebelde, liberándonos y diciendo gloomy los está esperando arriba es el la historia suicidándose." The Uncle's exit is now cold and direct. No comedy beats.

CUT

The Uncle enters. He is furious.
UNCLE: How is this possible. How are they still alive...
UNCLE: What is this. This is awful. Who made this coffee? (throws coffee at guard)
NINA: I dressed myself.
UNCLE: Go upstairs. Go do something appropriate. Go do girl things.
UNCLE: And these ones. What time are they executed?
BUNNY GUARD: In five minutes.
UNCLE: Make it three! And make it painful.

This entire exchange is replaced by the shoe-polish version above. The "go do girl things" beat is gone — Nina is no longer in pure-rebel mode. She's still inside his world, just starting to question it.

REWRITE — MAJOR

Two bunny guards step forward into the cage area, weapons raised. Crude blades. Spiked clubs. They take their positions in front of us, ready to finish the job.

Behind them, in the deeper darkness of the dungeon, something growls.

It is not the Uncle's pets. It's a different sound. Lower. Wilder. Feral.

Two glowing eyes appear in the dark.

The bunnies freeze. They turn slowly toward the sound. The growl gets louder. Closer.

One of the bunnies whimpers and steps backward into the shadows. He doesn't come back. We hear an impact. A short struggle. Silence.

The second bunny panics. He swings his weapon wildly into the dark — and is yanked off his feet by something we cannot see.

A sudden surge of force passes through the dungeon. We feel it on our faces, a hard wind that has no source. The torches blow out one by one, all the way down the corridor.

The dungeon goes black.

Then, in the darkness right next to our cage — the sound of an axe striking iron. Wood splinters. Metal cracks. The cage door swings open.

We don't see who freed us. We feel him standing there, just out of sight. Something powerful. Something we cannot quite look at.

Without words, we know what to do. He is moving — fast — and we follow the sound of his footsteps deeper into the dark.

Source: "Instead of the gravekeeper or Nina freeing us. It will be Gloomy! But we never really see him fully... feral noises... eyes appear from darkness... bunnies get scared and beaten... wind blows out torches... opens cage with stolen axe..." This is Gloomy's first appearance and he is intentionally hidden. The audience meets him as a force, not a face.

REWRITE — NEW SEQUENCE

We move through a narrow tunnel cut by hand from rough stone. Gloomy's tunnel — the way he came in. The walls are scratched with old marks. Someone has been using this passage for a long, long time.

The tunnel opens into a vast vertical shaft. A massive wooden freight elevator waits at the bottom — battered, ancient, big enough for all of us. We pile in.

Above us, the shaft disappears into darkness.

The elevator begins to rise. Slowly.

As we ascend, we pass through level after level of the Uncle's underground world. Each level shows us something we were never meant to see.

LEVEL — THE MINE

A massive mining operation. Dozens of bunny humans hacking at the rock walls, hauling carts of stone, digging deeper and deeper into the earth. They are looking for the sun. They have been looking for years. They will never find it. The work is constant, automatic, hopeless.

LEVEL — THE NIGHT MARKET

The bunnies' living quarters and a communal mess hall, lifted directly from a Taiwanese night market. Steaming food carts. Skewers. Dumplings. Stinky tofu. Noodles. The bunnies push their hoods up just enough to chew. For a moment they look almost human. Almost. They eat in silence.

LEVEL — THE GUARDS

A guard post. A bunny on duty looks up. Sees us in the rising elevator. Does a double-take. Sounds the alarm.

Suddenly, arrows begin flying at us from the surrounding platforms. We crouch low. Some hit the wood of the elevator. Some hit us — but we feel no pain. We are zombies.

Then — Gloomy moves.

We don't see him directly. We see what he does. A bunny on a platform falls, struck from behind by an unseen force. Then another. Then another. He moves through their ranks like a shadow, taking them out one by one in fast, precise attacks. We can track his movement only by the bunnies that drop.

The elevator keeps rising.

By the time we reach the top, the levels below have gone silent.

The shaft opens into stone. The stone opens into light.

For the first time in our existence, we step outside.

Source: "We walk through a small tunnel in the caves and we get to a large freight wooden elevator... we go up through many floors. We see the bunnies in a kind of mine where they are looking for the sun. We see how they live, how they eat (taiwanese night market) and when the bunnies realize we are escaping they start shooting arrows at us. Gloomy defends us by attacking them all from behind, in a ninja style sneak attack. We can follow his actions until we reach the top. We go outside for the first time."

Interactivity: Arrows fly toward us in the elevator — we can grab them mid-flight, pull them out of our zombie bodies, and throw them back. We feel no pain.

SCENE 02

First Light — Meeting Gloomy

Surface + River Crossing ~8 MIN
REWRITE

We are outside.

The elevator shaft sits behind us, its stone mouth slowly receding. The air is different up here — cold, damp, full of sounds we haven't heard in a long time. Leaves moving. Wind through trees. Something alive.

For a long moment, no one moves. We stand there, blinking at a world we forgot existed. There is no sun — the sky is grey — but it is vast.

We feel him. He's been with us this whole time, just behind us, just out of sight. Now, for the first time, he steps into view.

Gloomy.

He is small. Hunched. Half-feral. He moves like something that has been alone in the woods for too long. His eyes glow faintly. His body is tense, ready to bolt. The axe he used to free us is still in his hand.

He looks at us — fellow zombies — like he isn't sure if we're a threat or kin. He has been alone so long he doesn't remember what trust looks like.

He hisses softly. We don't move.

After a long, careful beat, he turns and gestures for us to follow. But he keeps physical distance. He won't let any of us get too close yet.

Source: Scene 02 reframed. Now that Gloomy already freed us in Scene 01 (unseen), Scene 02 becomes the moment we finally see his face. The "drops from above and fights bunnies" combat is gone — he already did all of that in the elevator. This scene is the cinematic reveal + the start of trust.

Gloomy leads us into a series of rocky tunnels that twist down toward the sound of water. The tunnels gradually open onto a small river, where a big wooden raft is waiting — clearly something he prepared.

The group boards the raft together. The current carries us forward and the caves begin to open up, revealing the forest of Alishan.

Tall trees line the river. What little light there is breaks through the canopy and reflects off the water. For the first time since leaving the dungeons, the world feels alive again.

Interactivity: Gloomy will gesture for us to shift our weight to one side or the other of the raft to steer correctly through the current.

SCENE 03

Gloomy's Home — Bad News

Refuge + Cemetery ~10 MIN

The group arrives at Gloomy's home. He built it himself over time, using whatever was around. Platforms, planks, and ramps stacked in uneven layers.

As we enter, a small wild boar suddenly runs toward Gloomy. For a moment it looks aggressive, but it quickly becomes clear they're playing. The boar bumps into him, circles around, and then calms down, wagging its tail before coming closer to sniff the group. The animal is clearly his pet.

Inside the house, drawings cover the walls and surfaces. Many of them show Nina. One drawing lies on a table and Gloomy awkwardly tries to hide it, embarrassed. Other drawings show a large glowing figure sending out beams of light, hinting at his dream of freeing the sun.

REWRITE

The group moves outside toward the nearby cemetery. The light in the forest dims slightly. The Gravekeeper is already there, kneeling beside an open hole in the ground. He's been digging.

Around him, on the dirt, are objects he has unearthed — or maybe he's about to bury them. A small bird skull. A faded photograph. A folded piece of paper. He doesn't put them back. He doesn't take them anywhere. He just keeps them in front of him, untouched.

Gloomy approaches him cautiously. The Gravekeeper looks up.

GRAVEKEEPER

So. You made it.

He looks past Gloomy, toward the forest.

GRAVEKEEPER

The Uncle knows what Nina did. He found out she helped you escape.
She's been captured. Locked away.

A pause.

GRAVEKEEPER

He's angry. He won't stop now.

GRAVEKEEPER

But Nina isn't the real reason you have to move.
The Uncle has built a machine. It runs on the train. The locomotive itself is the weapon —
it pulls energy from everything it passes through. From the roots. From the soil. From the sun.

He gestures toward the tracks in the distance.

GRAVEKEEPER

If you don't destroy that locomotive, there will be no sun to wake.
You have to board the train. Free Nina if you can. But the locomotive comes first.

He reaches for a bundle of old rabbit uniforms and hands them out.

GRAVEKEEPER

You can't move like this anymore. Not through his territory.
These will let you walk among them.

Source: "El gravekeeper nos da la misión, que tenemos que ir a destruir la locomotora." + "El entierra cosas sabe que pasó con el sol. Las entierra pero no las desentierra." — The mission is now clear and physical: destroy the locomotive. The Gravekeeper's gesture of digging things up but never taking them is the visual hint that he KNOWS more than he says.

CUT

Original: Gravekeeper just says "Nina has been captured" and gives uniforms. No mission. No locomotive. No reason to risk everything beyond rescuing one girl.

The mission was implicit. Now it's explicit: destroy the weapon that's killing the world. Rescuing Nina becomes a complication, not the goal.

A short training moment follows as the group tries to stand, move, and behave like humans. It is uncomfortable and awkward, but necessary.

Gloomy shares a disguise with the wild boar, standing on top of it to appear taller. He wobbles and struggles to keep his balance, making the whole attempt slightly ridiculous.

When the group is ready, the Gravekeeper steps aside and plays a short, unnecessary melody on his harmonica. It serves no real purpose.

In the distance, the sound of the train begins to approach.

Interactivity: We must copy the Gravekeeper's choreography to look human rather than zombies.

SCENE 04

The Train — Undercover

Alishan Forest Railway ~11 MIN

The group infiltrates the train disguised as rabbits. The platform is busy and tightly controlled. Soldiers move in lines, checking positions and watching for anything out of place.

Before boarding, the Gravekeeper pulls us aside for a final warning.

GRAVEKEEPER

The locomotive is at the front. Nina is being held in the very last wagon.
You'll have to choose. Or find a way to do both.
Follow Gloomy. He'll lead the way.

The train starts moving. Inside, the space feels narrow and tense. We follow Gloomy as he leads us through the wagons, bumping lightly into walls and door frames, clearly not built for this kind of disguise.

REWRITE

In one carriage, we encounter the Uncle. But this time, he isn't alone.

Nina is with him. She's standing close. Quiet. She doesn't look like a prisoner. She looks like someone who lives in this man's shadow and has lived there a long time. Her face is unreadable.

The Uncle is inspecting the strange mechanical device that hums softly and pulses with energy. He turns to Nina with sudden tenderness — the kind of tenderness that doesn't quite fit on his face.

UNCLE

Look at it, Nina. Look at what we're going to do.

UNCLE

When this is finished, the world will be ours again. Mine. Ours.
I'm going to bring back the sun. I am going to save the world.
And you'll be standing right next to me when it happens.

Nina says nothing. Her eyes flick — just for a second — to where we're standing in our disguises. She sees us. We see her see us.

UNCLE

You forgive me, don't you? For earlier? You know I only get angry because I love you.

It's the most exposed we'll ever see him. A flash of something almost human. Then it's gone.

Source: "El uncle es demasiado flat, necesita más color. Algo más con Nina. Momento vulnerable y medio weird al principio. El tío le dice a Nina que él va a salvar el mundo." — Three seconds of vulnerability, weaponized affection, and a self-image as savior. Now he's not a cartoon. He's a man who believes he's the hero.

The Uncle adjusts parts of the device, returning to his usual mode.

UNCLE

Too much glare. It looks wrong. Fix it.

Suddenly, he pauses and sniffs the air. His expression changes instantly.

UNCLE

What is that smell?
You stink! All of you stink! This is disgusting.

He screams that the rabbits should wash, that this filth is unacceptable. Without warning, he grabs one of the soldiers and kicks them out of the moving train.

The wagon goes silent.

Terrified of being discovered, we keep moving forward, staying close to Gloomy and avoiding eye contact.

At one point, the interior path is blocked. The only way forward is outside. We step onto the narrow exterior edge of the train as it cuts through the forest. The wind is loud, the ground far below.

One wrong step would mean falling.

SCENE 05

Nina — Magic Interrupted

Broken Crossing ~10 MIN

The group reaches the final wagon at the end of the train. Inside, Nina is imprisoned, guarded and restrained.

Gloomy moves in first and frees her. When he comes up behind her, Nina reacts on instinct and throws him to the floor. She freezes as she realizes who it is.

NINA

Wait. Gloomy?

She looks around and finally understands what is happening.

NINA

You actually came back. All of you!

NINA

They're coming, we have to move.

Without hesitation, she moves to the coupling mechanism and detaches the wagon from the rest of the train. The train continues forward while the wagon slows, rolling to a stop deep inside the forest.

We get out and move away from the tracks, but the path ahead is broken. Part bridge, part ruin, suspended over the forest ground.

Gloomy jumps first and lands on the other side. He turns back and reaches out to Nina.

When their hands touch, something changes.

REWRITE

For the first time, we see what happens when they connect. Light spreads across the broken structure. The bridge begins to rebuild itself through natural energy. Leaves lift into the air. Flowers open and release their petals, moving in slow, coordinated patterns around them.

And then — something unexpected. The wild boar, who has been trotting alongside Gloomy this whole time, suddenly lifts off the ground too. He floats upward, legs paddling in the air, completely confused. Gloomy notices him drifting away and gently reaches out, pulling the boar back down with one hand while still holding Nina with the other.

The boar lands on solid ground, snorts, and immediately puts himself between Nina and Gloomy. He looks up at Nina. He doesn't trust her. He stomps once, a small warning. Gloomy puts a hand on the boar's head and the boar grumbles but stays.

For a moment, everything is still.

Source: "Cuando Nina y Gloomy flotan por primera vez, el chochan también flota y lo baja a Gloomy. El chochan desconfía de Nina." — Comic relief that also seeds emotional conflict. The boar's distrust of Nina is a small visual reminder that not everyone is convinced — and later, this distrust will turn out to be earned.

Gradually, the light fades. Gloomy and Nina drift back down to the ground, landing on a surface now covered in dry leaves. Around us, movement stirs within the shadows. Huge eyes blink open in the darkness. The same terrifying barking and deep growls we heard in the dungeon echo nearby.

The Uncle's pets.

Their forms remain indistinct in the darkness, watching us, clearly hungry and ready to attack. Nina steps closer behind Gloomy.

NINA

Those things are wild. They're starving.
If we stay here, we're dead. There's no way out.

As the leopards move closer and escape seems impossible, a portal opens nearby. Through it, we can see another part of the world on the other side.

There is no time to hesitate.

We rush through the portal together.

Interactivity: We can touch the flowers as they pass nearby, causing them to release their petals.

SCENE 06

Pacha — What Still Lives

Pacha's Sanctuary ~12 MIN

We arrive inside Pacha's cave, a place full of life and movement. The space feels grown rather than built, like the inside of the Three Generation Tree. Roots twist along the walls and ceiling. Shelves are covered in vines, holding strange objects collected over time.

From between the roots, Pacha appears. She moves slowly, her body bent and fragile, like a very old woman. Her energy shifts constantly. One moment she is sharp and intense, the next she becomes sleepy and starts snoring mid sentence.

The wild boar trots straight to Pacha and rubs against her leg. She smiles for the first time and scratches his ears. They've met before. They know each other well.

Source: "El chancho y pacha se llevan muy bien."

PACHA

Oh. Look at you.
I was wondering when you'd show up.
Come closer, come closer.
You look tired…

PACHA

The sun is not gone.
Everyone thinks it is, but it is not.

She suddenly straightens, energized.

PACHA

It is still alive. Its energy never left.
It moved underground.

Her voice softens. She drifts.

PACHA

Beneath the forest. In the roots. In the trees…

She pauses, eyes closing. Nina gently pokes Pacha with a stick to wake her up. Pacha blinks, refocuses.

PACHA

Right. Yes! Alone, it cannot wake up.

She looks at the group.

PACHA

Humans and zombies. Together! That is the only way.

She shows us how to connect with the forest. How to activate its energy. We learn to call the souls of the trees and feel how nature responds when we act as a group rather than as individuals.

REWRITE

Pacha picks up two small rocks from a shelf woven into the roots. She holds them up.

PACHA

The portals you've been using —
They are not magic. They're not gifts.
They're cracks. Wounds in the world that opened when the sun left.

She strikes the rocks gently together. A small spark hangs in the air.

PACHA

When you pass through one, the world remembers you a little less.
That's the price. That's how they work.
Every door costs something.

Nina looks down. She has used many of these portals.

Source: "Que pacha explique biennn que son los portales." — Establishing portal rules. They have a price now. The audience can no longer assume they're a free escape.

As the lesson continues, fireflies gather, floating through the space and slowly moving toward the exit, guiding us outward.

REWRITE — MAJOR

Suddenly, we hear them coming.

Not the Uncle. The pets. The same terrifying barking from the bridge — closer now. Much closer.

And behind the barking: bunny soldiers. Heavy boots. Weapons.

Pacha turns to Nina. Her face is sad. Not surprised. Sad.

PACHA

(softly) They followed the trail.
The little marks. The footprints in the moss. The broken branches.
I know who left them, child.

Nina freezes. Her face goes white.

PACHA

It's all right.
It was supposed to happen this way.
Everything happens for a reason.

Nina starts to cry. Real tears. Years of them. The boar growls quietly at her — confirmation. He always knew.

The pets and the bunnies smash into the cave. Gloomy moves to defend Pacha. Pacha gently puts a hand on his arm.

PACHA

Not you. Me.

Pacha steps forward to meet the attack. She raises her hands and the roots of the cave respond — wrapping around the bunnies, slowing the pets. But there are too many.

A leopard reaches her first. She doesn't fight. She lets it.

As her body falls, she pushes one last wave of energy outward — into Gloomy. His eyes flash. He stumbles.

PACHA

(to Gloomy, weak) The seed. Carry it for me. Wake the sun.

Pacha collapses among the roots. The cave dims.

Gloomy lets out a sound none of us have heard from him before. Grief. Real, raw grief.

Nina opens a portal — and stops. She remembers what Pacha just said. She knows the cost.

She does it anyway. She pulls us through at the last possible second, escaping as the cave falls into darkness.

Source (Major Rewrite): "Usar mucho los perros, que en la 6 no aparezca y que pacha sea atacada por perros y bunnies. Nina deja pistas a los perros. Gravísimo." + "she's ancient from sacrifice, se sacrifica por ellos, vemos como la atacan. pacha sabe que nina estuvo dejando pistas, que 'trabaja' para los malos. Pacha la consuela y le dice que es lo que tenia que pasar. que todo sucede por algo. Nina llora." — This is the biggest single change in the script. The Uncle is no longer in this scene. The perros and bunnies do the attacking. Nina's secret betrayal is exposed not through accusation but through Pacha's compassion. Pacha sacrifices herself instead of being a passive victim. The wild boar's distrust from Scene 05 is paid off here.

CUT

The Uncle attacks again, forcing his way into the space. He raises his weapon and uses it directly on Gloomy, draining energy from his eyes. Gloomy collapses to the ground. NINA: Nooooooo!!!! Believing him dead, Nina opens a portal and pulls us through.

The Uncle is no longer in this scene. The pets + bunnies + Nina's betrayal carry the weight. Gloomy is wounded by Pacha's dying transfer of energy, not by the Uncle's weapon.

Interactivity: We must raise and lower our hands, as Pacha does, allowing particles of light to connect us with the natural environment.

SCENE 07

The Mountains — Everything Falls Apart

Miniature Paths — Mountain Cliffs ~7 MIN
REWRITE

We emerge into a world covered in fog. The air is cold and heavy, and sound feels distant.

Nina collapses. Not figuratively — physically. Her knees give out. She sits down on the wet ground and doesn't get up.

Gloomy is barely conscious from Pacha's energy transfer, lying in the moss. The boar stands between Nina and Gloomy, alert.

No one is leading.

For a long time, nothing happens. The fog moves around us. The mountain breathes far away. We are alone with what we just lost.

Then — small lights. The fireflies. Just a few. They start to drift through the fog, away from us, deeper into the mountains. Toward something.

Nina doesn't look up.

The boar looks at us. The Last Standing. He waits.

One of us has to take the first step. (This is the audience's first true act of leadership. There is no music. There is no narration. Only the choice to walk.)

When the group finally begins to move, the world responds. The miniature paths appear — small versions of mountains, valleys, cliffs, and ruins forming and reforming in the fog ahead of us. We are walking through impossible distances.

The Gravekeeper's voice carries softly over the scene, almost as a memory.

GRAVEKEEPER (V.O.)

When in doubt… follow the fireflies.

Behind us, eventually, we hear footsteps. Nina has stood up. She is following us. Not leading. Following. Because the people she betrayed didn't leave her behind.

Source: "Scene 07 Is Narratively Hollow — buenísimo lo que propone." (referring to the Solutions section rewrite) + "Ante la duda sigan a las luciérnagas." — The audience leads for the first time. Nina follows, broken but redeemable. The fireflies guide. The miniature world becomes the sound of distance, not a visual gimmick.

The miniature scene fades. We are back at full scale, in the mountains, following glowing footprints.

The path narrows until it becomes a thin trail along a cliff edge. Below us, the ground disappears into fog. Ahead, the path opens into a massive cavern carved deep inside the mountain.

Light begins to appear through the fog. Clear beams break through gaps in the rock. The mist thins, revealing the scale of the space.

NINA

(quietly) It's here. The sun is down here.

Before anything else can happen, the Uncle storms in, forcing his way into the cavern. He turns directly to Nina.

UNCLE

You chose him over me. And every step you took in his direction brought me exactly where I wanted to be.

The ground begins to shake. Cracks spread across the stone beneath our feet. The Uncle advances, raising his weapon. He strikes the ground and the stone fractures around us.

UNCLE

Walk!! You're already dead anyway. Keep moving!!

The mountain continues to collapse around us. There is no time left.

SCENE 08

Final Confrontation — Awakening the Sun

The Sun Cavern ~10 MIN

The Uncle prepares to activate his machine. The device hums louder, pulling energy from the void below.

Suddenly, a portal opens above at 45 degrees. Gloomy returns through the portal in a wide, uncontrolled jump. He lands hard between the Uncle and Nina, barely holding himself upright. His body is damaged. His eyes burn — but with Pacha's light now, not just his own.

UNCLE

You again! That cannot be. You were dead.

Gloomy does not hesitate. He attacks immediately, driven by raw, uncontrolled energy. His eyes burn red. His hair lifts and moves like fire. Each impact shakes the ground beneath them.

The fight is violent and chaotic. The Uncle tries to defend himself, but Gloomy pushes him back again and again, forcing him toward the edge.

Nina moves forward, trying to stop it.

NINA

Stop! Please. Stop.

The Uncle turns and strikes her, throwing her to the ground.

UNCLE

Stay out of it.

Something breaks in Gloomy. With one final surge of energy, he charges forward and throws the Uncle into the abyss below. The sound disappears into the darkness.

REWRITE

Just before he goes over the edge, the Uncle looks at Nina. For half a second, his face is not the tyrant's face. It's the face of the man who said "I am going to save the world." It's the face of someone who really believed it.

Then he's gone.

Source: Three seconds of human moment for the villain, paid off. From "El uncle es demasiado flat, necesita más color." His fall is sad, not just satisfying.

Gloomy collapses immediately after, drained and motionless.

Nina crawls toward him, barely able to stand. She reaches him and holds him for a moment. Then she looks up at us.

NINA

Do it! What you learned. Now.

She raises her hands and shows us the movement. Slow and deliberate. The same gesture we practiced before. She repeats it, urging us to follow.

We mirror the motion together.

The mountain responds.

Thousands of fireflies rise from the ground and the walls, swirling through the air before pouring into Gloomy. Light spreads through his body.

Slowly, he awakens.

The mountain begins to rebuild itself. Cracks close. Stone settles back into place.

Behind them, the sun rises for the first time. Light floods the cavern.

Gloomy and Nina hold each other as the world around them changes.

Interactivity: We must raise and lower our hands, as Nina does, allowing particles of light to connect us with the natural environment.

OUTRO

A New Dawn

Shadow Miniature Epilogue ~3 MIN

The Gravekeeper's voice returns, calm and distant, echoing gently through the space.

GRAVEKEEPER

The sun has returned.

Light spreads across the land. The air feels warmer.

GRAVEKEEPER

Nature is breathing again.

Animals slowly emerge from the forest. Birds begin to sing as the light grows stronger. Night recedes, giving way to morning.

GRAVEKEEPER

You walked where nothing was meant to survive.
You stood together when everything was falling apart.

REWRITE

A pause. He looks toward the cave where Pacha fell.

GRAVEKEEPER

Not all of us came back.
Some of us gave the last of what we had so this morning could exist.

He looks toward the horizon. In one direction, the new sun rises and the forest is alive. In the other direction — far away, beyond the mountains — the sky is still dark. Still dead.

GRAVEKEEPER

This world is fragile. It always was.

A faint smile.

GRAVEKEEPER

But today, it lives.
For now.

The light spreads — but not everywhere. A new dawn begins. The work isn't done.

Source: "ENDING — They resolve enough to give hope, and leave enough unresolved to give the story teeth." — Pacha is acknowledged as dead. The sun rises here, but not everywhere. "For now" is no longer poetry. It's a warning.
