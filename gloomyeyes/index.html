The Premise

A World Without Sun

Long ago, the sun retreated into the mountains. Without its light, the land rotted — forests weakened, animals vanished, and the delicate balance between humans and nature collapsed. From that darkness, zombies emerged: not monsters, but consequences.

Under the iron rule of The Uncle, fear was weaponised into hatred. Survivors were imprisoned underground. The last of the zombies — you, The Last Standing — await execution.

One chance remains. A quiet young zombie named Gloomy is waiting above. And somewhere beneath the mountain, the sun still breathes.

The Cast

Characters

☠

Gloomy

Protector & Guide

A quiet, strange, brave zombie — essentially child-like. Carries simultaneous sadness and curiosity. He survived years alone, learning to listen to an erased natural world. His connection with Nina is simple and instinctive, like two children. Driven by courage, sacrifice, and a deep desire to restore balance.

ZombieProtectorSilent
✦

Nina

The Rebel Human

Guided by innocence and intuition. A strong heart, a rebellious spirit, and an empathy rare in the Uncle's world. She respects zombies when no one else does. She frees prisoners, defies authority, and becomes the key force in the world's rebirth. Brave, impulsive, deeply human.

HumanRebelliousEmpathetic
★

The Uncle

The Authoritarian Ruler

Egocentric, narcissistic, obsessed with control, purity, and appearances. He sees himself as the centre of the world — comparing his power to the sun's. Possessive of Nina, disguising domination as protection. Beneath his grandiosity lies deep insecurity and paranoia. His authority is enforced through fear, humiliation, and absurd commands.

VillainAuthoritarianParanoid
▦

The Gravekeeper

The Philosopher

Strange, mysterious caretaker of forgotten places. Part poet, part philosopher, quietly bohemian. He observes the world with distance and reflection, understanding the fragile balance between humans, zombies, and nature. He speaks with a calm ritual tone — enigmatic yet guiding at key moments, offering knowledge and warnings without stepping into conflict.

EnigmaticPoeticGuide
❀

Pacha

Ancient Forest Woman

Ancient, wise, deeply connected to nature's hidden systems. Her body is intertwined with roots, plants, and colourful fungi growing from her skin. She moves with extreme slowness and frequently falls asleep mid-sentence — resuming exactly where she left off when awakened. Her wardrobe reflects native Alishan communities; her presence embodies ancestral knowledge and living memory.

AncientNatureAlishan
♾

The Last Standing

The Audience — You

The last surviving zombies. You experience this world as newcomers — learning through movement, instinct, and collective action. You run, hide, fight, and reconnect with nature alongside Gloomy and Nina. You are not spectators. You are essential parts of the world's rebirth. Without you, the sun stays buried.

ZombiePlayerEssential

The Wild Boar

Gloomy's Companion

Young, small, agile, and fearless. Gloomy's loyal companion who moves quickly through the forest and never hesitates to protect his friend. He brings warmth, humour, and life into Gloomy's isolated world — and stands tall when it matters most, even letting Gloomy use him as a mount during disguise training.

The Uncle's Guards

Ordinary humans wearing rabbit-like uniforms as a sign of devotion to the Uncle's cult. Completely submissive, following despotic orders and repeating ridiculous speeches without question. Initially ghostly and intimidating, their appearance becomes subtly absurd — their long pointed hoods resembling bunny ears reflect the hollow, twisted nature of the regime they serve.

The Script

Scene by Scene

INTRO

The Hiding of the Sun

⸻ Darkness. A distant warmth pulses from afar.

The Gravekeeper emerges slowly from shadow, telling the story of a world unmade. Narrated with small puppets that transform into living, self-moving characters. The sun retreated into the mountains. Without light, the land rotted. Zombies emerged as consequence, not as monsters. Fear bred hatred. Survivors were imprisoned. One chance remains: find Gloomy.

Prologue
01
EXECUTION ORDER

The Dungeons

⸻ Underground cages. Humans in rabbit costumes guard zombie prisoners.

The Uncle enters enraged. He demands faster executions — three minutes, with pain. Nina arrives; a tense confrontation reveals his possessive obsession. Massive unseen creatures respond to his command with explosive barking and glowing eyes. A single clap silences them. Then Nina strikes a guard, seizes a key, and forces open the door.

"Someone is waiting for you above." — Nina
▶ Players can grab arrows, pull them out, and throw them at others
02

First Escape: Gloomy

⸻ Surface. Bunny soldiers closing in.

Gloomy drops from above — silent, swift, precise. He circles the group, forcing soldiers back without a word. A brief struggle. A magenta burst of light. He returns holding a soldier's white hood; the body left behind. The others panic and scatter. Gloomy gestures for the group to follow, leading through tunnels to a small river where a wooden raft waits. The current carries them into the Alishan forest — tall trees, light through canopy, the first suggestion of restored life.

▶ Players help shift the raft's position to guide it correctly through the current
03

Gloomy's Home: Bad News

⸻ An improvised dwelling — stacked platforms, planks, and ramps.

The wild boar appears, playfully engaging Gloomy before calming to sniff the group. The walls are covered in drawings — many featuring Nina. One on the table causes Gloomy visible embarrassment; he hides it quickly. Others depict a large glowing figure sending light beams into the world. Then the Gravekeeper returns with grave news: Nina has been captured and locked away. They must board the Uncle's train.

A training sequence begins — the group must learn to walk, move, and behave like humans. Gloomy perches atop the wild boar for height, wobbling comically. The Gravekeeper plays harmonica. A train rumbles in the distance.

▶ Players must copy the Gravekeeper's choreography to pass as human
04
HIGH TENSION

The Train: Undercover

⸻ A moving train. Narrow, tense interior spaces.

Nina is held in the very last wagon. The group follows Gloomy through carriage after carriage of bunny soldiers, moving carefully, avoiding eye contact. Then the Uncle appears — inspecting a mechanical device that hums with extracted sun energy. He suddenly sniffs the air. Zombie. He explodes, grabbing a soldier and kicking them from the moving train. The carriage goes silent. The group presses forward. The interior path becomes blocked. The only way forward: a narrow ledge along the exterior of the train, wind roaring, the ground far below.

05

Nina: Magic Interrupted

⸻ The final wagon. Nina imprisoned and guarded.

Gloomy frees Nina. She throws him to the floor instinctively — then recognises him. "Wait. Gloomy?" She detaches the wagon from the rest of the train. The separation is sudden. As the train disappears ahead, the wagon rolls to a gentle stop in the forest. On a broken bridge over forest ground, Gloomy jumps across and reaches back toward Nina. The moment their hands touch, light spreads across the structure — the bridge rebuilds through natural energy. Leaves lift, flowers open, petals fall in slow coordinated patterns. Then the darkness stirs. The Uncle's pets have found them.

"Those things are wild. They're starving. If we stay here, we're dead." — Nina

A portal opens. The group rushes through together.

▶ Players can touch passing flowers to trigger petal releases
06

Pacha: What Still Lives

⸻ A cave full of life — grown, not built. Roots twist through walls and ceiling.

Pacha moves slowly, energy shifting between sharp intensity and sudden sleep. Nina pokes her with a stick to keep her awake. Her revelation: the sun is not gone. It moved underground — into the roots, into the trees, alive within the forest itself. Alone, it cannot wake. Only humans and zombies together can restore the balance.

"Alone, it cannot wake up. Humans and zombies. Together! That is the only way." — Pacha

She teaches the group how to connect with the forest's hidden energy. Fireflies gather and lead the way to a portal. Then the Uncle breaks in — raises his weapon and drains the energy from Gloomy's eyes. Gloomy collapses. Nina screams, opens the portal, and pulls the group through at the last second.

▶ Players raise and lower hands like Pacha, allowing light particles to connect with the natural environment
07
COLLAPSE

The Mountains: Everything Falls Apart

⸻ A world covered in fog. Cold, heavy air. Distant sound.

"Gloomy is gone." Nina stands still for a moment, shaken — then keeps moving. The world shrinks into a miniature sequence: portals open and close as small figures cross forests, cliffs, valleys, and ruins. The Gravekeeper narrates the miles and miles traveled through forgotten paths. Then the world expands back to full scale. Glowing footprints lead the way forward. The path narrows to a cliff's edge — and then opens into a massive cavern carved inside the mountain. The air changes. Light breaks through gaps in the stone.

"It's here! The sun is down here!!" — Nina

The Uncle storms in. The ground cracks beneath them. The mountain begins to collapse.

"Walk!! You're already dead anyway. Keep moving!!" — The Uncle
08
AWAKENING

Final Confrontation: Awakening the Sun

⸻ The Sun Cavern. A mountain collapsing around you.

The Uncle's device hums, draining the void below. Then a portal opens at a 45-degree angle above — and Gloomy falls through it in a wide, uncontrolled arc, landing hard between the Uncle and Nina. Damaged. Barely upright. His eyes burn red; his hair lifts like fire. The fight is violent and chaotic. Nina tries to stop it. The Uncle strikes her to the ground. Something breaks in Gloomy. He charges — one final surge — and throws the Uncle into the abyss. Then collapses.

"Do it! What you learned. Now." — Nina

Nina raises her hands. The same slow gesture Pacha taught. She urges the group to follow. Together, thousands of fireflies rise from the ground and walls — swirling through the air, pouring into Gloomy. Light spreads through his body. Slowly, he awakens. The cracks close. The stone settles. For the first time in living memory, the sun rises.

▶ Players raise and lower hands with Nina — their collective motion awakens the sun
OUTRO

A New Dawn

⸻ Post-conflict. Light spreading across the land.

The Gravekeeper speaks one final time. The sun has returned. Air feels warmer. Nature breathes again. Animals slowly emerge; birds begin singing as the light grows stronger. Night recedes. Morning arrives for the first time in a long, long while.

"This world is fragile. It always was. But today, it lives. For now." — The Gravekeeper
Epilogue
Participation

You Are Not a Spectator

The Last Standing is an audience-driven experience. Every action you take has consequence. The world responds to what you do — together.

▶

Movement Choreography

Mirror the Gravekeeper's movements precisely to pass as human through enemy territory. Get it wrong, and the disguise fails.

✨

Light Particle Control

Raise and lower your hands in sync with the forest's energy. Your gestures directly activate hidden light within the natural world.

→

Arrow Manipulation

Grab arrows mid-flight, pull them out, and throw them. Zombies feel no pain — use that to your advantage.

♨

Raft Navigation

Shift your weight to guide the raft through underground river passages toward the light of the Alishan forest.

⚘

Flower Triggers

Touch the flowers that drift past on the broken bridge. Each contact releases a cascade of petals — a moment of magic between Gloomy and Nina.

☀

The Final Awakening

The most important action in the experience: raise your hands together with Nina. Without the collective motion of every single person, the sun stays buried forever.

Rooted in Place

Taiwan Cultural Elements

Gloomy Eyes & The Last Standing is deeply embedded in the landscapes, wildlife, infrastructure, and indigenous heritage of Taiwan — specifically the Alishan mountain region.

🌳

Alishan Forest

The primary landscape draws from Alishan — Taiwan's iconic high-altitude forest of giant cypress trees, layered canopy, mountain presence, and characteristic mountain fog. The foggy environments that permeate the later scenes directly reference Alishan's distinctive climate.

🚂

Alishan Forest Railway

The iconic red mountain trains of the Alishan Forest Railway — a historic narrow-gauge line built during the Japanese colonial era — serve as the setting for Scene 4. The train infiltration sequence is a direct homage to this beloved piece of Taiwanese infrastructure.

🦅

Formosan Wildlife

Gloomy's loyal companion, the wild boar, references the Formosan wild boar — a keystone animal of Taiwan's mountain ecosystems. The Uncle's terrifying unseen pets are inspired by the Formosan leopard cat, one of Taiwan's rarest native predators.

🏭

Tsou Indigenous Heritage

Pacha's wardrobe is drawn directly from the traditional dress of the Tsou people — an indigenous group native to the Alishan region. The bows and arrows used throughout the experience reference traditional Tsou hunting tools, and Pacha's cave is inspired by Alishan's renowned Three Generation Tree.

🌎

Sound & Atmosphere

Bird sounds throughout the experience are inspired by native Taiwanese species. Interior mountain spaces reflect Taiwan's geological formations. The layered soundscape of forest, fog, rain, and distant wildlife grounds the fantastical story in a deeply specific, real place.

🎄

Fireflies

Fireflies appear as a recurring motif — guiding the group through Pacha's sanctuary, rising from the earth in the final confrontation, and ultimately awakening Gloomy. Firefly watching is a beloved cultural tradition in Taiwan's mountain forests, particularly around Alishan each spring.
