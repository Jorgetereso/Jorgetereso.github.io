CHAPTER I

A Note

We do not arrange tours. We compose journeys — slow, particular, assembled across years of friendships with chefs, monks, gardeners, and innkeepers who do not advertise. Each is written by hand, for one party at a time.

"We are slow on purpose. The unrushed traveller sees what the others cannot."

We accept twelve a year. The rest, regrettably, we decline. What you find on these pages is not a catalogue — it is a doorway. Press it gently and write to us; we will reply, slowly and in full sentences.

CHAPTER III

The Journeys

III IN COMPOSITION · MMXXVI

III  ·  i

Kyoto, after hours.

Tea with a seventh-generation master, taken in a room that smells of cedar and rain. A geiko's salon, doors closed to all but the introduced. Kaiseki at a counter with five seats, where the chef will not speak unless you ask. Dawn at a Zen garden no guidebook prints, with a monk who agreed only on the third visit.

"This city does not perform for visitors. It performs for itself, and one is permitted to watch."

VII NIGHTS  ·  SPRING — AUTUMN
III  ·  ii

The Kumano Kodō.

A pilgrim's path through cedar forests, walked at the pace it was meant to be walked — twelve kilometres a day, never more. Cypress baths at the day's end, water hot enough to remember. Suppers cooked over irori in mountain ryokan, eaten in unhurried silence. Mornings at shrines older than any guidebook in any language.

"The mountain is not a destination. It is the teacher."

V NIGHTS  ·  SPRING — AUTUMN
III  ·  iii

Bespoke, by request.

By correspondence. Write to us with a sentence about who you are, where you have already been, what you love, and what you have never been able to find. We reply with three propositions, in long form. The journey is then composed for you alone — a process that takes, on average, four months.

CHAPTER IV

A Correspondence

Begin with a letter. Tell us your name, where in the world you write from, and a thing you have been trying to see. We reply, slowly and in full sentences, within two days. From the first exchange to the day of departure is rarely less than four months — that is how the work is done.
